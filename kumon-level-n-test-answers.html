Question 2: Polynomial Factoring

Topic: Factoring higher-degree algebraic expressions.

Concept: Applying synthetic division or grouping methods to find rational roots of complex polynomials.

Question 3: Rational Expressions

Topic: Simplifying fractional polynomials and stating domain constraints.

Concept: Factorizing numerator and denominator to cancel common factors. The denominator must never equal zero.

Question 4: Fractional Inequalities

Topic: Solving inequalities involving rational expressions.

Concept: Setting up boundary intervals and testing sign changes on the coordinate number line.

Question 5: Nonlinear Systems

Topic: Intersection points of lines and curves.

Concept: Equating system equations to solve for variables coordinates, checking for real vs. complex roots.

Question 6: Arithmetic Progressions

Topic: Calculating nth terms and sums of arithmetic sequences.

Formula: $a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$ and $S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n)$.

Question 7: Geometric Sequences

Topic: Finding common ratios and geometric progression terms.

Formula: $a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}$. Essential foundation for exponential behaviors.

Question 8: Summation Notation

Topic: Working with Sigma ($\Sigma$) series parameters.

Concept: Evaluating summation bounds using standard series properties for linear and quadratic terms.

Question 9: Infinite Series Convergence

Topic: Convergence conditions for infinite geometric series.

Concept: Evaluating whether a series has a finite sum boundary based on the ratio magnitude $|r| < 1$.

Question 10: Limits of Rational Functions

Topic: Evaluating function limits as $x$ approaches a finite value.

Concept: Factoring out indeterminate $\frac{0}{0}$ terms to resolve the limit boundaries.

Question 11: Infinite Limits

Topic: Limits as independent variables approach positive/negative infinity.

Concept: Dividing terms by the highest degree power in the denominator to isolate leading coefficient behaviors.

Question 12: Instantaneous Rates

Topic: Concept of derivative as instantaneous slope tangent.

Concept: Finding the limiting value of secant slopes as the distance $\Delta x \to 0$.

Question 13: Power Rule Differentiation

Topic: Direct differentiation of polynomial expressions.

Formula: $\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = n x^{n-1}$. Applied term-by-term across algebra functions.

Question 14: Quotient and Chain Rules

Topic: Advanced rules of differentiation for fractional functions.

Formula: $\frac{d}{dx}[\frac{u}{v}] = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$.

Question 15: Critical Points & Extrema

Topic: Identifying local maximum and minimum values.

Concept: Setting the first derivative equal to zero ($f'(x) = 0$) and checking signs.

Question 16: Applied Optimization

Topic: Real-world optimization functions using derivative parameters.

Concept: Setting up functions to maximize/minimize parameters like volume, area, or efficiency under constraints.

Question 17: Indefinite Integrals

Topic: Antiderivatives of polynomials.

Concept: Reversing differentiation rules and remembering to add the constant of integration ($+ C$).

Question 18: Definite Integrals & Area

Topic: Evaluating exact areas under curves.

Concept: Calculating integrals over bounds $[a, b]$: $\int_a^b f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a)$.

Question 19: Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Topic: Relationship between differentiation and integration.

Concept: Differentiating an integral function: $\frac{d}{dx} \int_a^x f(t)dt = f(x)$.

Question 20: Comprehensive Review

Concept: Combines functions graph behaviors with differential calculus techniques. Excellent final review.
