To successfully obtain your certificate and move on to the final Level O, you must demonstrate proficiency in graphing functions, dealing with locus coordinates, solving polynomial sequences, and evaluating mathematical limits.

Mathematical Breakdowns for Level N

Key Topic: Limits of Functions

Evaluating the limit as $x \to a$ of a rational function. If direct substitution results in an indeterminate form ($\frac{0}{0}$), you must factorize the numerator and denominator, cancel common terms, and then substitute the value. This technique is extensively tested on Sheet 2.

Key Topic: Infinite Geometric Series

For a geometric progression with first term $a$ and common ratio $r$, the infinite sum exists if and only if $|r| < 1$. The sum is given by the formula:

S = a / (1 - r)

Problems on the Level N test verify your ability to compute values of $r$ from sequences and solve for $S$.

Key Topic: Finding Locus of Points

Understanding geometric locus. Sheet 1 displays problems where you must represent coordinate inequalities. Remember to verify boundary conditions (whether boundaries are included or excluded in the quadratic region).
